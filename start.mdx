Choose your starting path based on your role.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Medical Writers" icon="pen-line">
    ### For freelance or in-house medical writers

    **Read first:**
    1. [Risk Levels](/principles/risk-levels) — which of your deliverables fall into which tier
    2. [Human-in-the-Loop](/principles/human-in-the-loop) — the review framework for every AI-assisted output

    **Start with these workflows:**
    1. [Summarise a Source Paper](/workflows/summarise-source-paper) — low risk, mirrors work you already do
    2. [Extract Key Messages](/workflows/extract-key-messages) — medium risk, saves time in early-stage content development
    3. [Build a Content Outline](/workflows/build-content-outline) — low risk, works for any deliverable type

    **Before anything leaves your desk:**
    - [Final Human Review](/workflows/final-human-review) — structured QC checklist for AI-assisted content

    **Most relevant tools:**
    - [RefCheckr](/tools/refcheckr) — claim verification in multi-reference documents
    - [PosterLens](/tools/posterlens) — rapid congress poster extraction

    **What this gives you:**
    A documented, quality-controlled process. Instead of "I used AI to help draft this," you can say: "I followed a structured workflow with defined review steps, source verification, and a documented QC checklist."
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Agency Teams" icon="building">
    ### For agency leads, team leads, and operations managers

    **Read first:**
    1. All four [Principles](/principles/human-in-the-loop) — assess how they map to your SOPs and QC processes
    2. [Review and Accountability](/principles/review-and-accountability) — sign-off protocols and audit trails

    **Match workflows to your project types:**
    - **Publication support:** [Summarise a Paper](/workflows/summarise-source-paper) → [Key Messages](/workflows/extract-key-messages) → [Content Outline](/workflows/build-content-outline)
    - **Promotional content:** [Content Outline](/workflows/build-content-outline) → [Compliance Check](/workflows/check-promotional-compliance) → [Verify Claims](/workflows/verify-claims-against-references)
    - **Congress coverage:** [Poster Summary](/workflows/prepare-congress-or-poster-summary) → [Repurpose Across Channels](/workflows/repurpose-content-across-channels)
    - **Patient-facing:** [Plain Language Summary](/workflows/create-plain-language-summary) → [Final Review](/workflows/final-human-review)

    **Key tools for your team:**
    - [RefCheckr](/tools/refcheckr) — pre-MLR reference verification
    - [MedCheckr](/tools/medcheckr) — compliance screening to reduce MLR rejection cycles
    - [LLMentor](/tools/llmentor) — multi-audience adaptation from a single approved source

    **For client conversations:**
    The [Risk Levels](/principles/risk-levels) framework and [Compliance Check](/workflows/check-promotional-compliance) workflow are the key credibility documents when briefing clients on AI-assisted work.
  </Tab>

## Recommended reading order

Regardless of role, this is a practical path through the playbook:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Understand the foundation">
    Read [Human-in-the-Loop](/principles/human-in-the-loop) and [Source Grounding](/principles/source-grounding).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Learn the risk framework">
    Read [Risk Levels](/principles/risk-levels) — which tasks carry which level of risk.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try a low-risk workflow">
    Start with [Summarise a Source Paper](/workflows/summarise-source-paper). It demonstrates the full workflow card structure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build up to higher-risk workflows">
    Work through [Extract Key Messages](/workflows/extract-key-messages) and [Adapt for Audiences](/workflows/adapt-for-different-audiences).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Always finish with review">
    Run every AI-assisted deliverable through [Final Human Review](/workflows/final-human-review).
  </Step>
</Steps>
